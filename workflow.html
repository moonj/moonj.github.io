Workflow Case Study

Workflow was initially conceived as an app for creators first – that is, people would spend more time creating workflows rather than downloading them. Of course, this ended up not being the case, and we discovered that the Workflow Gallery – essentially, our App Store – was a crucial way for new users to get acquainted with the app. It also hadn’t been updated in 1.5 years. We wanted to redesign the Gallery with a year and a half of user feedback in mind.

The Gallery had been infrequently updated, which limited how capable people perceived Workflow to be. Journalists writing about the app – who, like many users, looked to the Gallery for example use cases for the app – would cite the same sample workflows over and over.

We wanted to turn the Gallery into something that looked alive – something people wanted to check for ideas to use Workflow more often and in more areas of their life.
